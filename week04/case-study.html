Business Case: Retail Rhapsody Customer & Product Analysis

The Story

Retail Rhapsody is a small but growing electronics retailer that specializes in the latest gadgets and accessories. Over the past few years, the company has built up a diverse customer base and a carefully curated inventory of products. However, the owner—Ms. Rivera—has noticed that while overall sales are steady, there may be untapped opportunities to tailor marketing efforts and optimize inventory based on customer spending habits and product availability.

To make data-driven decisions, Ms. Rivera has asked you, the business analytics team, to analyze the company’s customer and product data. She believes that by understanding which customers are the most valuable and which products are consistently in stock, Retail Rhapsody can design targeted promotions that maximize revenue and customer satisfaction.

The Problem

Your task is to analyze the provided datasets using Python. You will need to work with the concepts covered in the first three weeks of our course—variables, data types, lists, dictionaries, tuples, sets, and control flow (loops and conditionals).

Specifically, you are asked to:

  1. Identify High-Value Customers:
    • Find and print the names of customers who have spent more than $100.
  2. Calculate Total Revenue:
    • Compute the total revenue generated from all customers by summing their spending.
  3. Compute Average Spending:
    • Determine the average amount spent per customer.
  4. List Unique Customer Cities:
    • Create and print a set of all unique cities where the customers reside.
  5. Analyze Product Stock:
    • From the product data, identify which products are currently in stock.
    • Print the details (product name, product ID, and price) of these in-stock products.
  6. (Bonus Challenge)
    • Sort the list of products by price in ascending order and print the sorted list.
